About the academy

Fifty-two years of urban-forest science.

The Academy was established in 1973 to support faculty, students, and researchers conducting and disseminating arboricultural and urban forestry research.

Our mission

We help the science of trees travel.

We encourage communication and coordination among the scientists and educators working in arboriculture and urban forestry — across the physical, biological, ecological, and social sciences.

The goal is simple: make it easier for good work to move. A method developed in one lab, a result from one city, a question raised in one classroom — all of it travels further when the field is connected.

The mark

A leaf and a microscope.

Look closely at the wordmark. The first A holds an oak leaf; the last A holds a microscope. Between them, the green deepens from new growth to old.

It's the whole mission in four letters — the tree, and the science that studies it — and it's where this site takes its colors.
